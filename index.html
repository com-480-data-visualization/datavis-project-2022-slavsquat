ABOUT

Barcelona is a city on the coast of northeastern Spain. It is the capital and largest city of the autonomous community of Catalonia, as well as the second most populous municipality of Spain.

Barcelona districts

To conduct our analyses better, we focused the data around the ten districts of Barcelona. The districts are: Ciutat Vella, Eixample, Gracia, Horta-Guinardo, Les Corts, Nou Barris, Sant Andreu, Sant Marti, Sants-Montjuic, and Sarria-Sant Gervasi. We provide a map of districts so you can get familiar with their position.

Accidents

On the pie chart below, you can preview the number of accidents that happened in specific districts. You can filter out the the accidents that happened throughout different parts of the day: Morning, Afternoon, and Night. It could be observed that the highest percentage value for accidents is for the Eixample district, the center district of Barcelona.

On the bubble chart below, the x-axis represents the total number of accidents that happened in a district. On the other hand, we decided for the y-axis to be the accidents that happened in the afternoon since most accidents in Barcelona happened in the afternoon. The size of the bubbles, which represent different districts, is determined by the number of victims for accidents that happened in the afternoon.
